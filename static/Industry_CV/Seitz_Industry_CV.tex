\small{
Developing genomic large language models (gLMs) and other AI-driven approaches for genomic research, focusing on interpretable machine learning methods for regulatory genomics.

\textbf{\href{https://www.cshl.edu/}{Cold Spring Harbor Laboratory}}, Postdoctoral Fellow with Drs. \href{https://www.cshl.edu/research/faculty-staff/peter-koo/}{Peter Koo} and \href{https://www.cshl.edu/research/faculty-staff/justin-kinney/}{Justin Kinney}\\
\href{https://www.cshl.edu/research/quantitative-biology/}{Simons Center for Quantitative Biology} \hfill March 2022 – September 2025

\small{
Developed a surrogate modeling framework—\href{https://github.com/evanseitz/squid-nn}{SQUID}—to mechanistically interpret deep neural networks trained on genomic data, enabling robust dissection of cis-regulatory logic in high-throughput functional assays.

    \begin{itemize}
        \item Modeled additive and higher-order interactions between sequence features while removing confounding effects from nonlinearities and experimental noise using shallow neural networks as interpretable surrogates.
        \item Demonstrated SQUID identifies more consistent motifs across genomic loci and improves single-nucleotide variant effect prediction, outperforming established attribution methods like DeepSHAP.
    \end{itemize}

Invented a meta-explainability framework—\href{https://github.com/evanseitz/seam-nn}{SEAM}—that systematically investigates how mutations reshape regulatory mechanisms, enabling interpretable insights into mutational effects on gene regulation.

    \begin{itemize}
        \item Enabled unbiased discovery of mutations poised to reprogram cis-regulatory mechanisms and function, illuminating evolutionary trajectories and supporting the rational design of synthetic regulatory sequences.
        \item Distinguished sequence features that are robust to mutation from those that are readily reprogrammable, enabling visual separation of motif-driven signals from biologically important background patterns.
        \item Demonstrated SEAM reduces noise and better highlights regulatory features than other attribution methods.
    \end{itemize}
}


\textbf{\href{https://www.columbia.edu/}{Columbia University}}, PhD Researcher with Nobel laureate Dr. \href{https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/chemistry/2017/frank}{Joachim Frank}\\
\href{https://biology.columbia.edu/}{Departments of Biological Sciences} \& \href{https://www.biochem.cuimc.columbia.edu/}{Biochemistry and Molecular Biophysics} \hfill May 2017 – February 2022

\small{
Invented a geometric machine learning method—\href{https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/9773954}{ESPER}—to interpret latent manifolds derived from cryo-EM ensembles of macromolecular complexes, enabling accurate reconstruction of 3D structures across the free-energy landscape.

    \begin{itemize}
        \item Improved the interpretability of spectral embeddings by introducing a novel eigenbasis realignment procedure that extracts conformational degrees of freedom along eigenfunctions of the Laplace–Beltrami operator.
        \item Enabled reconstruction of conformational continua and free-energy landscapes in key biomolecular systems, including the 80S ribosome, V-ATPase, and SARS-CoV-2 Spike protein.
    \end{itemize}
}
